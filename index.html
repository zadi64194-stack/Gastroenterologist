When to Act

Signs You May Need to See a Gastroenterologist

Many people wait too long before booking an appointment with a digestive health specialist. They may assume the discomfort will go away on its own or believe their symptoms are not serious enough for expert care. In reality, certain warning signs should always be taken seriously.

  • Frequent heartburn or acid reflux that does not improve
  • Ongoing abdominal pain or stomach cramps
  • Constipation or diarrhea that lasts for days or weeks
  • Blood in the stool or black-colored stool
  • Difficulty swallowing or pain while eating
  • Unexplained weight loss and reduced appetite
  • Persistent bloating, gas, or nausea
  • Family history of colon cancer or digestive disease

These signs do not always indicate a severe illness, but they do suggest that a professional evaluation is necessary. Early diagnosis can make treatment easier, faster, and more effective.

Patient Journey

What to Expect During a Gastroenterology Visit

Patients who search for Dr. Gina Sims gastroenterologist often want to know what happens during an appointment. In most cases, the first visit begins with a complete discussion of symptoms, medical history, lifestyle habits, medications, and family health background. The doctor may ask when symptoms started, how often they occur, and whether certain foods or activities make them worse.

After this discussion, the specialist may recommend physical evaluation, laboratory tests, imaging, or procedures such as endoscopy or colonoscopy depending on the symptoms involved. These tests help provide a clear picture of digestive function and allow the doctor to make an accurate diagnosis.

The most important part of the process is individualized care. No two patients have the exact same symptoms or medical history. A skilled gastroenterologist creates a treatment plan based on the person’s condition, comfort, and long-term health goals.
